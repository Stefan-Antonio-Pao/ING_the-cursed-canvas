Before the First Step

After Midnight, the Frames Began to Whisper

The rain had stopped by the time you reached the museum steps. You had only meant to retrieve something left behind after closing, but the final gallery light went dark before you found your way back out.

Then the doors locked themselves. Moonlight spilled across the marble floor, and somewhere beyond the central hall a frame gave a soft, impossible crackle, as if canvas were taking a breath.

A note waited on the curator's desk: the museum's paintings were losing their colors, one world at a time. If no one stepped inside before dawn, the curse would spread from frame to frame until every doorway became a wall.

You are alone in the midnight gallery, with only the whispering paintings to guide you. The first frame shimmers. The journey begins when you choose to cross it.
